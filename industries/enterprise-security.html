Why private security needs BWC

Security work is now a liability business.

Every confrontation your guard has with a customer, a contractor, or an intruder is a potential lawsuit, viral video, or insurance claim. Without body worn cameras, every narrative is contestable — and the burden of proof falls on the business.

Private security deployments across the UK, US, and Australia have consistently shown that a visible BWC reduces aggression, accelerates incident resolution, and produces evidence that holds up in court, insurance tribunals, and internal reviews.

The Risk Landscape

What's actually going wrong
on your security floor.

These are the recurring incident patterns security directors see across Indian retail, corporate, hospitality, and SEZ deployments.

Assaults on security staff

Retail and hospitality guards face verbal abuse and physical assault daily. UK ACS 2023 data: 87% of retail workers experienced verbal abuse in the past year; 22% physical violence. Indian numbers trend similarly in malls, QSRs, and transit hubs.

Retail shrinkage disputes

A loss-prevention stop with no video is a lawsuit waiting to happen. Global retail shrink reached $112.1B in 2022 (NRF), up 19.5% YoY — and the fastest-growing component is external theft at the point of apprehension.

Guard accountability

Your guard posts have no ongoing visibility. Sleep-on-duty, unauthorised absences, and missed patrol rounds cost Indian security agencies an estimated 15-20% of billed man-hours (industry estimate, FSAI 2023).

Training gaps, unknown behaviours

You don't know how your guards actually handle incidents until something goes wrong. Without recorded footage, training is abstract. BWC footage becomes the highest-signal training dataset your agency has ever had.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions your legal,
ops, and procurement teams will ask.

Do body worn cameras reduce assaults on security guards?

Yes. UK retailer deployments of BWCs for guards reported 40-60% reductions in verbal and physical confrontations. Morrisons supermarket recorded a 60% drop after equipping customer-facing staff with body cameras in 2020-21.

How do body cameras reduce retail shrinkage?

BWCs deter confrontation-based theft at the point of apprehension, produce indisputable evidence of shoplifting, and resolve false claims against loss-prevention staff. The UK Association of Convenience Stores (2023) found 89% of retailers using BWCs reported reduced incident disputes.

Are body cameras legal for private security in India?

Yes, provided the recording occurs in public or semi-public areas where no reasonable expectation of privacy exists (shop floors, lobbies, perimeters). Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, organisations must display notice of recording, limit retention, and secure the data. Prosight One meets all DPDP technical requirements.

Does Prosight One integrate with PSARA-compliant security agencies?

Yes. Prosight's evidence management platform logs guard identity, deployment site, and incident records in a format compatible with PSARA (Private Security Agencies Regulation Act) audit requirements. Training modules are available for supervisors and guards.

Can the live feed be shared with a corporate command centre?

Yes. Prosight One streams live 2K video over 4G LTE or site WiFi and supports RTSP integration into existing VMS/SoC platforms like Milestone, Genetec, and indigenous Indian command platforms.

What happens to the data? Where is it stored?

Data is stored on-device (256GB, encrypted) and synced to either Prosight Cloud (hosted in Indian data centres — Mumbai, Hyderabad availability zones) or your own on-premise server. Data sovereignty stays in India. Retention is configurable per your compliance policy.
