What is a Police Body Worn Camera

The definition, in one paragraph.

A police body worn camera (BWC) is a shoulder- or chest-mounted recording device that captures audio-video footage of an officer's interactions with the public. Modern BWCs like the Prosight One also stream live 2K video over 4G LTE to a command centre, log GPS coordinates, and apply AES-256 encryption with GPS-stamped watermarks for court-admissible chain of custody.

Across 18+ randomised controlled trials globally, BWCs have consistently reduced use-of-force incidents, civilian complaints, and assaults on officers — while accelerating complaint resolution and improving prosecution success rates.

The Accountability Gap

The problems Indian policing
can no longer ignore.

Without video evidence, every incident becomes a contested narrative. These are the operational realities that force commanders across the country are grappling with today.

He-said-she-said disputes

Custodial allegations and public-interaction disputes drag on for months without corroborating evidence. The National Human Rights Commission registered 2,152 custodial deaths between 2019-2023 (NHRC Annual Report, 2023) — many contested without video.

Weak evidence at the scene

Witnesses turn hostile. CCTV coverage is spotty in semi-urban and rural areas. Conviction rates for IPC crimes in India sit at roughly 57% — and for crimes against women, conviction remains under 27% (NCRB, 2022). Primary evidence gaps are the single largest cause.

Officer safety, zero visibility

Commanders can't see what their officers are facing in the field. During 2022, 188 police personnel were killed on duty in India (BPR&D data, 2023), with many incidents escalating because command was blind to the situation in real time.

Broken public trust

The CSDS-Common Cause Status of Policing in India report (2023) found that only 25% of citizens have "a great deal" of trust in police. Every viral incident without video erodes institutional credibility — and recruitment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Your procurement team
asked. Here are the answers.

Do body worn cameras actually reduce police use of force?

Yes. The landmark Rialto, California randomised controlled trial (Cambridge University, 2013) found a 59% reduction in use-of-force incidents and an 87% reduction in civilian complaints when officers wore body cameras. Subsequent RCTs across Las Vegas (−25% use of force), Spokane, and UK forces have replicated double-digit reductions across both metrics.

Are body worn cameras used by Indian police?

Yes. Delhi Police, Hyderabad Police, Kerala Police, Punjab Police, and Uttar Pradesh Police have all rolled out BWC programmes between 2018 and 2025, with deployments ranging from 2,000 to 12,000 units per force. The Supreme Court of India directed states to consider BWC for custodial transparency in the 2020 Paramvir Singh Saini v. Baljit Singh judgment.

How long does a Prosight One body worn camera record?

A fully-charged Prosight One records continuously for 10-12 hours at 1080p30 or 8-10 hours at 2K. It supports hot-swap batteries for back-to-back shift coverage and has 256GB on-device storage with H.265 compression — sufficient for an entire shift without upload.

Is body worn camera footage admissible as evidence in Indian courts?

Yes. Under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (which replaced the Indian Evidence Act), electronic records including body worn camera footage are admissible as evidence provided chain-of-custody integrity is maintained. Prosight One uses AES-256 encryption, GPS-stamped watermarking, and SHA-256 hash verification to preserve evidentiary integrity.

What is the IP rating of Prosight body worn cameras?

Prosight One is IP68-rated — fully dustproof and capable of withstanding immersion in water up to 1.5m for 30 minutes. It also survives 2-metre drops and operates from −20°C to +60°C, making it suitable for monsoon, desert, and high-altitude deployments across India.

How are body camera recordings protected from tampering?

Prosight One applies AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit. Each recording is watermarked with device ID, officer ID, GPS coordinates, and UTC timestamp. Files are cryptographically hashed on device; any modification breaks the hash and is flagged. USB access and device menus are password-protected.

Can body cameras be used for live command-centre monitoring?

Yes. Prosight One streams live 2K video over 4G LTE (12+ bands) or WiFi to the Prosight command dashboard in under 3 seconds of latency. Commanders can view multi-camera grids, track GPS positions on a live map, and push-to-talk to individual officers or cluster groups.
