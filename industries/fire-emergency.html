Why BWC on the fireground

Fires don't wait for radio calls.

Incident commanders make life-or-death decisions — search, ventilate, evacuate — with fragmented radio reports from crews inside smoke-filled structures. A body worn camera changes that. The commander sees what the firefighter sees, in real time, from every angle of the scene.

BWC footage also transforms post-incident review from memory-based debrief into evidence-based learning. Near-misses, PPE failures, and tactical errors become teachable artifacts for the next generation of crews.

The Fireground Problem

Where visibility gaps
turn into tragedies.

Command is blind inside the structure

The IC makes calls based on radio descriptions alone. When interior conditions change — flashover signs, structural compromise, trapped occupants — delays cost lives. NFPA 1500 repeatedly identifies inadequate situational awareness as a contributing factor in firefighter fatalities.

Post-incident review is memory-based

The after-action report is assembled from crew recall — days later, with trauma and adrenaline distorting sequence. Tactical lessons are lost. Near-misses go under-reported. Training programmes are built on guesswork instead of footage.

Arson evidence contaminated by suppression

By the time investigators arrive, hoses and boots have rearranged the origin. The first-arriving firefighter's eyes were the purest witness — and without BWC footage, that viewpoint is lost forever.

Crew accountability on mass-casualty scenes

At high-rise fires and industrial incidents with 30+ firefighters across multiple sectors, commanders lose track of who went where. BWC location reporting and live video close that gap.

Frequently Asked Questions

Operational questions
from fire chiefs.

Are body worn cameras used by fire services?

Yes. FDNY (New York) has deployed BWCs since 2016; Chicago Fire Department deployed BWCs in 2022; UK services including West Midlands Fire Service and London Fire Brigade run active BWC programmes. BWC footage is increasingly used for incident command review, firefighter training, and arson investigation.

Can Prosight One survive fireground conditions?

Prosight One is IP68-rated, drop-tested to 2 metres, and operates from −20°C to +60°C. It is not a self-contained breathing apparatus-integrated camera, so deployment inside an IDLH atmosphere requires a dedicated thermal-rated mount or helmet-cam variant — we supply both as part of the fire-service kit.

How is BWC footage used in arson investigation?

BWC footage captures the first-arriving officer's view of fire origin, spread pattern, occupant behaviour, and evidence on scene before suppression contaminates it. The captured video is timestamped and GPS-tagged, providing a contemporaneous record admissible under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023.

Does BWC improve firefighter safety?

Yes, primarily through better command decisions. When commanders see what crews see in real time, they make more informed evacuation and ventilation calls. Post-incident review of BWC footage also surfaces safety protocol gaps that lead to systemic training improvements.

What happens to BWC recordings after an incident?

Recordings auto-upload to Prosight Cloud (Indian data centres) or your on-premise evidence server over WiFi when units return to station. Chain-of-custody is preserved with SHA-256 hashing, AES-256 encryption, and immutable audit logs.
