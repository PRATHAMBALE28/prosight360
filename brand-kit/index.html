04 · Voice & Tone
Direct. Factual. No fluff.
Prosight sells to police commissioners, CISF officers, and enterprise security heads. They respect evidence. They distrust hype. Every word we write should earn their trust, not perform confidence.
Principle 01
Factual before rhetorical
Every claim we make has a source. We cite NHRC, DRI, IMA, CAPSI, BSF Annual Reports. If we don't have data, we say we don't have it. We never fabricate urgency.
Principle 02
Evidence, not assertion
We don't claim "the best" or "industry-leading." We state what the M530 does, at what spec, verified by whom. Cambridge 2014. UK Met 2017. NSW Police 2015. The research does the selling.
Principle 03
Respect for the professional
Our buyer has 20 years in uniform or enterprise security. We write for their intellect, not past it. We explain, we don't oversell. We acknowledge gaps where they exist.

Copy examples — in practice

✓ Prosight voice
"DRI and CBIC seize over ₹12,000 crore in contraband each year. Without GPS-stamped field video, seizure chains are reconstructed from memory — and routinely challenged at the first cross-examination."
Specific number, named agency, specific consequence. Reads like a briefing document, not a brochure.
✓ Prosight voice
"On the honest roadmap: on-device face blur and medical-clip retention policies are Phase-2 capabilities — not live today. We will not ship them until they are right."
Acknowledging a gap builds more trust with a professional buyer than hiding it ever would.
✕ Not Prosight
"Our revolutionary AI-powered body cameras are transforming law enforcement across India with industry-leading features and cutting-edge technology."
No numbers, no citations, no specifics. "Revolutionary" and "industry-leading" are claims without evidence. A police commissioner closes the tab.

Post content guidelines

✓ Share
  • Research findings with citation (Cambridge 2014, UK Met 2017)
  • Sector statistics with source (NHRC, CAPSI, IMA)
  • Deployment context — problem → ProSight solution
  • Regulatory updates (DPDP Act, PSARA amendments)
  • Honest product updates with real specs
✕ Never post
  • Unverified claims ("our cameras are used by 50+ forces")
  • Competitor comparisons without data
  • Stock photography presented as ProSight deployments
  • Marketing jargon: "revolutionary", "next-gen", "AI-powered"
  • Testimonials without written client permission

07 · Usage Rules
What the logo is never allowed to do.
Six rules. No exceptions. The logo is a legal and brand asset — treat it like one.
Don't stretch or distort
Never scale non-proportionally. Always hold shift when resizing.
Don't recolour
Use only Jet Black (#0A0A0A) or Off-White (#FAFAFA). No other colours.
Don't add drop shadows
The logo is a flat mark. No shadows, glows, or 3D effects of any kind.
Don't place on coloured backgrounds
Logo lives on: off-white, white, navy (#0B1629), or black (#0A0A0A) only.
Don't rotate or skew
The logo is always horizontal and upright. No angles, tilts, or perspective transforms.
← too small →
Don't use below minimum size
Minimum 120 px wide (digital) or 32 mm wide (print). Below this the mark is illegible.
One final rule
"Security, Not Surveillance."
This tagline is a commitment, not a slogan. Every product decision, every marketing message, and every sales conversation should be consistent with this principle. If in doubt, ask: does this make our users feel watched, or protected?
